# Global Growth & Expansion Playbook

This document frames the strategies for aggressive scale: building, buying, or partnering to conquer new markets.

## 1. The Growth Triad

### A. Organic Growth (Blitzscaling)
*   **Concept:** Prioritizing speed over efficiency in an environment of uncertainty.
*   **When to use:** When you have a "Winner Take All" digital product (e.g., a platform or software).
*   **Technique:**
    *   **Network Effects:** Engineer your product so every new user makes it better for others.
    *   **Cash Burn:** Accept short-term massive losses to capture market share (e.g., Uber style). **Warning:** Requires deep pockets or massive venture backing.

### B. Franchising & Licensing
*   **Concept:** Using other people's money (OPM) to build your distribution.
*   **When to use:** Physical businesses (retail, service) or IP-heavy brands.
*   **Structure:**
    *   **Master Franchise:** You sell the rights to a whole country to one partner. They sub-franchise and deal with local headaches. You take a royalty cut.
    *   **Benefits:** Rapid scale, zero capital expenditure (CAPEX) for you, local expertise baked in.
    *   **Risks:** Loss of brand control. You need a fierce QA inspection team.

### C. Mergers & Acquisitions (M&A)
*   **Concept:** Buying the innovation or market share.
*   **The Playbook:**
    *   **The "Bolt-On":** Buying a competitor in a new geography to instantly gain their customers.
    *   **The "Acqui-hire":** Buying a small failing company just to get their brilliant engineering team.
    *   **The "Roll-Up":** Buying 10 small, fragmented players in a boring industry (e.g., HVAC, waste management) to create one giant dominant player.

## 2. Global Market Entry Operating Models

### The Beachhead Strategy
Don't invade "Europe." Invade "London."
*   **Focus:** Pick one narrow segment or city in a new territory. Win it completely.
*   **Expand:** Use that credibility and cash flow to expand to the neighboring city/sector.

### "Make in India, Sell in America" (Arbitrage)
*   **Geographical Arbitrage:**
    *   **Cost Centers:** Locate labor-intensive units in low-cost regions (India, Vietnam, SA).
    *   **Revenue Centers:** Locate sales/marketing in high-purchasing-power regions (USA, EU, UAE).
*   **Tax Arbitrage:** Structure corporate HQs in business-friendly jurisdictions (Singapore, Delaware, Mauritius) while operations remain local.

## 3. The "Kill" Criteria

Expansion is dangerous. Have pre-set rules for when to retreat.
*   **The 18-Month Rule:** If a new market isn't contribution margin positive in 18 months, exit.
*   **The "Distraction" Test:** If a new venture takes 20% of the CEO's time but offers only 2% revenue potential, kill it.
